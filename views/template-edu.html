Red Planet

Fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System after Mercury.

Mars is one of the four inner — or terrestrial — planets. These planets are composed primarily of silicate rock and metals and have solid surfaces.

  • Equatorial diameter: 6,792 km
  • Mass: 6.42 x 10^23 kg
  • Moons: Phobos and Deimos
  • Orbit period: 687 days (1.9 years)
  • First recorded: 2nd millennium BC
  • Atmosphere: Mostly carbon dioxide

Red Planet

The reddish brown appearance gives Mars its nickname "Red Planet," which also hearkens to the planet's true namesake, the Roman god of war, with the color reminiscent of blood.

Largest dust storms

Mars has been known to have planet-covering dust storms that last for months. This may be due to its extra elongated orbit generating extreme seasonal patterns.

Competing with Saturn

Mars' largest moon, Phobos, is estimated to be torn up in the next 20-40 million years, leaving Mars with a ring like Saturn.

Highest mountain in the solar system

Mars is home to the shield volcano Olympus Mons, which is 21km high, and may still be active.

How big is Mars?

Mars is 15% of the volume of Earth.
